Median Income % Change 2010 vs. 2015

There has been an increase of median household income for all wards in Toronto from 2010 Canada Census to 2015 Canada Census. Ward 28 Toronto Centre-Rosedale has the highest increase of 23.5%, ward 20 Trinity-Spadina has an increase of 21.7%, ward 16 Eglinton-Lawrence has an increase of 19.7, and ward 14 Parkdale-HighPark is just right behind with 19.4% increase.

The wards with the lowest increases are ward 23 Willowdale with only a 0.5% increase, ward 24 Willowdale with 3.8% increase and ward 44 Scarborough East with an increase of 6.6%.

Toronto Ward Comparsion of Marjor Crimes

Below is a map of Major Crime Indicators (MCI) from the Toronto Police Force from 2014 and from 2018.

The Major Crime Indicators (MCI) dataset we used only includes Robbery, Break and Enter, Assault, Theft Over, and Auto Theft.

The crime rate has increased by 15% from 2014 to 2018. The highest crime neighbourhoods are Church-Yonge Corridor, Waterfront Communities-The Island, West Humber-Clairville and Moss Park. The crime rate increased is 54%, 36%, 28%, and 48% respectively.

The neighbourhoods with the highest rate of increase are Rexdale-Kipling with 83%, Mimico (includes Humber Bay Shores) with 76%, Bathurst Manor with 61% and Elms-Old Rexdale with 59%. However, the actual crime count in these arears are actually quite low with 183, 557, 261, 135 and 285 respectively.

The neighbourhoods with the biggest decrease in crime was Woodbine-Lumsden with -29%, Ionview with -30% and O'Connor-Parkview with -33%, but again the crime in these arears are relatively low to start with 86, 124 and 170 respectively.
